About me

My name is Anna Pot. I hold a PhD in language and cognition from the University of Groningen, the Netherlands. My research identifies the contribution of language to (cognitive) development in old age. I have written a dissertation about this entitled Aging in a multilingual Netherlands. More specifically, this research uncovers whether, and how, speaking multiple languages contributes to enhanced cognition at an advanced age. Currently, I work as a data scientist.

This page highlights current projects and projects that I have worked on in the past.

Projects

Language, aging and cognition

How does speaking multilple languages contribute to cognition at an older age? In my dissertation, I focused on whether we can define in what way multilingualism contributes to healthy aging, among a range of other variables. I found that multilingualism should be regarded a continuum, not binary, and that only when you actively use different languages on a daily basis, cognition benefits.

These findings are published in following articles:

  • Pot, A., Porkert, J., & Keijzer, M. (2019). The bidirectional in bilingual: cognitive, social and linguistic effects of and on third-age language learning. Behavioral Sciences, 9(9), 98.
  • Pot, A. (2019). Aging in multilingual Netherlands: Effects on cognition, wellbeing and health. Rijksuniversiteit Groningen.
  • Pot, A., Keijzer, M., & De Bot, K. (2018). Intensity of multilingual language use predicts cognitive performance in some multilingual older adults. Brain Sciences, 8(5), 92.

Aging in a second language environment

When people migrate and age in a linguistic environment that differs from their home language, how does this impact their health and wellbeing? My research shows that a different language environment contributes to lower perceived wellbeing and health in a group of older, female Turkish migrants in the Netherlands.

These findings are published in the following articles:

  • Pot, A., Keijzer, M., & De Bot, K. (2018). The language barrier in migrant aging. International Journal of Bilingual Education and Bilingualism, 23(9), 1139-1157.

Do IT: diversity in Dutch L2 materials

How is ethnic diversity represented in widely-used textbooks of Dutch as an L2 (or Lx)? This is a project, led by Dr. Dietha Koster of the University of Munster, in which colleages in London, Jakarta and Prague participate. Findings will be expected in Spring 2022.
